Problem 1: (5 pts) The data set Titanic is not tidy. Suggest a different way to represent this data set that would be tidy. What columns would you need? What would the individual rows represent?

There are two possible answers to this question:

To make Titanic tidy, there should be one column for sex, one column for age, one column for survival, and one column for class. Each row would represent an observation for one individual.

To make Titanic tidy, there should be one column for sex, one column for age, one column for survival, one column for class, and one column for count. Each row would represent an observation for a group with a unique combination of sex, age, survival, and class variables.

Problem 3: (40 pts)

a. (30 points) Using the flights data set, find the mean distance and mean air time in each month for flights from the three New York airports. Now make one plot that visualizes all this information at once. Your code should be well-commented and describe the various steps you take to create this figure.

flights %>% 
  group_by(month,origin) %>%
  summarize(mean_air_time=mean(air_time,na.rm=T),mean_dist=mean(distance,na.rm=T)) -> flights_summary

ggplot(flights_summary,aes(x=month,y=mean_air_time,color=origin)) + 
  geom_line()+
  geom_point(aes(size=mean_dist))+
  scale_size(guide = guide_legend(title = "Mean distance"))+
  scale_color_discrete(guide = guide_legend(title = "Airport"))+
  xlab('Month')+
  ylab('Mean air time')+
  scale_x_continuous(breaks=1:12)

b. (10 points) Discuss the information (overarching trends, patterns, etc.) your final plot reveals. Be sure to include in your discussion the similarities/differences among the three New York airports and a clear, logical justification for why you selected the particular geom(s) used to represent this data. Please limit your full response to a maximum of 6 sentences.

I used geom_point() to plot one mean air time for each month, to indicate mean distance with point size, and to separate airports with color. I used geom_line() to capture the trends in mean air time over time. The mean air time and distance are the greatest for flights from John F. Kennedy International Airport (JFK), and they are the smallest for LaGuardia Airport (LGA) with flights from Newark Liberty Internation Airport (EWR) in between. For all airports, mean air time increases with mean distance. The longest flights for JFK and EWR are taken in December. For LGA, the longest flights are taken in winter months.
